ABOUT

Feral: A Journal of Ozarkian Gothic is a locally-published collection of art and stories celebrating, elaborating upon, or spinning anew tales of the Ozarkian weird.

Brooke Austin is an immortal, having been granted this boon by the Devil as the result of a rather heated game of backgammon that took place in a Wal-Mart parking lot when she was twelve. More Info

Local changeling, Karlei Baker, was recently confirmed rare but not endangered by the Missouri Dept. of Conservation. More Info

Sandi Baker's secret ambition of being Christian County's premier serpent handler was trumped by her success as an artist and art teacher. The snakes of Missouri are better off that way, and so are the rest of us.

When Todd Balisle was 12-years-old, he signed a written vow that he would one day become either a Power Ranger or a member of the nWo Wolfpac. He now works as a technical writer in Springfield, MO. More Info

Dae Barker is ten years old.

K.W. Barrett is some real cheese. He will obey. He will Obey. He will obey. More Info

Bryan Batsell has an abiding love for art, rock'n'roll and dead things. He also enjoys roof-climbing and socks. He is the winner of two Citation(s) of merit and one Citation of Distinguished Merit during shows at the University of Central Missouri, where he earned his BFA in sculpture. More Info

Chris Bently is a writer and illustrator from Canton, Ohio. He helps to create the literary magazine INSOMNIA & OBSESSION. More Info

Clay Cantrell received his MFA in poetry at the University of Memphis. In 2015, he moved to Tulsa to pursue a Ph.D. in Literature at UT. His poems have appeared or are forthcoming in Sycamore Review, New Delta Review, Birdfeast and The Journal. His full-length manuscript, Hermit, Wraith, was recently a finalist for the St. Lawrence Book Award. Another collection, The landfill Poems, will be published by Red Dirt Press in 2016. More Info

Jason and Chelsea Childress live in Mentor, Missouri and enjoy preserving, reinterpreting murder ballads, and cleaning skeletons. More Info

Zachary Crownover is a fickle courtesan to media en vogue. Unlike Silas Misener, he actually has been struck by lightning.

Randolph Dietz is a freelance writer, artist and educator from Southwest Missouri, living on the farm where he raised four children and many reptiles. More Info

It has been said that Lisa Erhart's writing is like the first cigarette of the day -- you know that it's killing a small part of you, but you can't put it down. Her first piece was published in Chicken Soup for the Soul.

Amelia Fisher is a student at Missouri State University, where she practices becoming a brooding novelist who "works" from home. You can often find her hanging out with her pets or listening to true crime podcasts.

Josh Holland, son of the Ozark Hills, channels dark visions in ink from an apartment in Kansas City, MO. He loves his cat, and illustrates Dark Circle Comics.

Aaron Ivie is an artist with a BFA in printmaking. Drawing influence from works of Dali and M.C. Escher, he channels his creative abilities into crafting salads and cheese breads in the restaurant industry. More Info

Elaine Johnson's roots lie in Missouri where she currently works as a freelance artist. She holds a BFA in traditional drawing and painting, and is the recipient of the Springfield Cider Days' Outstanding Local Artist of 2015 award. When she is not busy with commission work, she enjoys engaging in quality conversations with her dogs, Zeus and Stretch, and playing fetch with her boyfriend, Aaron. More Info

Jessica Lower has lived in the Ozarks for over 15 years. Working in a multitude of mediums, the majority of her works are portraits that often feature dull colors, impossible situations, and eerie atmospheres. She believes there is something alluring in odd and uncomfortable images, and so works to produce pieces that express such subtle beauty. More Info

Kat Minx was reared in the woods near a cistern by a gun-slinging moonshiner.

Silas Misener is a figment of your imagination. He certainly does not make his living tending bar in a town you forgot on your way to forget a larger town. He has never been attacked by a mountain lion or struck by lightning, and his hobby is unquestionably not the search for Elvis' Cadillac-Stonehenge.

Matthew Mitchell is an Ozarks native. He writes Dark Circle Comics and reviews horror films under the pseudonym, "Dirk Carcle." More Info

Patrick "Patsquatch" Needham was once regularly exhibited in the Kansas City Cryptozoological Museum, but was forcefully removed and released back into the wild due to his all-too-frequent verbal attacks on patrons in attempt to sway their opinions on Nihilist theory and cast iron skillets. More Info

Midge Pickett was raised on half-formed duck embryos and pig blood, and loves to bathe in the juice of overripe melons until her skin turns soft and wrinkly. More Info

Sara Pilz's pockets contain a half-brain of rock and a drawing of a demented rabbit drawn by her eight-year-old cousin. Her paintings are inspired by her wonderment of extraterrestrials, the natural world, single-celled organisms and memories of Atlantis. More Info

Dillon Rudder, having found fame and glory in the Great Fossil Wars, has retired comfortably to rural Christian County, MO, where he raises fightin' snakes. More Info

Davey Saturn is a fellow leaf in the gutter, just waiting to be hosed out. He enjoys long walks with his dog, Cider. More Info

Bill Shultz is a part-time farmer, part-time barista, part-time bartender, amateur poet with a Bachelor's, amateur painter with a Master's, and a full-time curious guy who lives in Springfield, MO...part of the time. More Info

Daniel Zender is a freelance illustrator and artist working in Brooklyn, NY. He grew up in Springfield, MO. Clients include The NY Times, New Yorker, and others. More Info

SUBMISSIONS

Submitters should emphasize writing or artwork pertaining to darker themes and situations within the Ozarks and send their file to feralozarkiangothic@gmail.com. Any format is welcome. Editors reserve the right to talk shit, edit-ta-death, or flat out refuse your chickenscratch.
